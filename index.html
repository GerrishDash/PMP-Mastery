🗺️ Your Study Roadmap

The PMP exam (2021+) tests on Predictive (Waterfall), Agile, and Hybrid approaches. About 50% of the exam is predictive and 50% is agile/hybrid. Here's how to study:

Step 1

📘 Master Process Groups & Knowledge Areas

Learn the 5 Process Groups (Initiating → Closing) and 10 Knowledge Areas with their 49 processes from PMBOK 6th Edition.

Predictive
Step 2

🧭 Internalize the 12 Principles

Understand the principle-based standard from PMBOK 7th Edition: Stewardship, Team, Stakeholders, Value, Systems Thinking, Leadership, Tailoring, Quality, Complexity, Risk, Adaptability, and Change.

Principles-Based
Step 3

🎯 Learn 8 Performance Domains

Understand how the 8 domains interact as a system: Stakeholders, Team, Development Approach & Life Cycle, Planning, Project Work, Delivery, Measurement, and Uncertainty.

Domains
Step 4

⚖️ Compare Predictive vs Agile

Know when to use which approach. Understand Scrum ceremonies, Kanban, servant leadership, and how hybrid combines both worlds.

Hybrid
Step 5

📐 Master EVM Formulas

PV, EV, AC → CV, SV, CPI, SPI, EAC, ETC, VAC, TCPI. Know how to interpret each metric and what actions to take.

Calculations
Step 6

🧪 Practice Scenario Questions

The PMP exam is situational. Practice "What should the PM do NEXT?" and "What is the BEST action?" questions.

Exam Prep

📖 Key PMBOK 6 Definitions
Definition

Project

A temporary endeavor undertaken to create a unique product, service, or result. Has a definite beginning and end.

Definition

Program

A group of related projects, subsidiary programs, and program activities managed in a coordinated way to obtain benefits not available from managing them individually.

Definition

Portfolio

Projects, programs, subsidiary portfolios, and operations managed as a group to achieve strategic objectives.

Definition

OPA — Organizational Process Assets

Plans, processes, policies, procedures, and knowledge bases specific to and used by the performing organization. Internal to the org.

Definition

EEF — Enterprise Environmental Factors

Conditions, not under the project team's control, that influence, constrain, or direct the project. Can be internal or external.

Definition

Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)

A hierarchical decomposition of the total scope of work to be carried out by the project team. The lowest level is a Work Package.

⚖️ Predictive vs Agile vs Hybrid

The PMP exam is approximately 50% predictive and 50% agile/hybrid. Know the differences, when to use each, and key ceremonies.

🏗️ Predictive (Waterfall)
Approach
Plan-driven; requirements defined upfront
Scope
Fixed scope; changes controlled via CCB
Schedule
Sequential phases; Gantt charts, CPM
Delivery
Single delivery at end of project
Documentation
Comprehensive; detailed PM plan
Change
Formal change control process
Team Structure
Hierarchical; PM directs work
Best For
Well-understood, stable requirements (construction, manufacturing)
🚀 Agile (Adaptive)
Approach
Value-driven; requirements evolve through collaboration
Scope
Flexible scope; prioritized product backlog
Schedule
Iterative sprints (1–4 weeks); velocity-based
Delivery
Incremental; working product each sprint
Documentation
Just enough; user stories, backlog
Change
Welcomed; backlog reprioritized each sprint
Team Structure
Self-organizing; servant leader (Scrum Master)
Best For
Evolving requirements, innovation (software, R&D)
🔄 Hybrid
Approach
Combines predictive & adaptive elements
Scope
Some fixed, some flexible components
Schedule
Phased with iterative elements within phases
Delivery
Mix of incremental and single deliveries
Documentation
Moderate; tailored to project needs
Change
Formal for some areas, flexible for others
Team Structure
Combined; PM + Scrum Master may coexist
Best For
Large projects with mixed certainty levels
🔁 Agile / Scrum Key Ceremonies & Roles
Ceremony

Sprint Planning

Team selects backlog items for the sprint; defines the Sprint Goal and how work will be done. Timeboxed to 8 hours (for 4-week sprint).

Ceremony

Daily Standup (Daily Scrum)

15-minute timebox. Team answers: What did I do? What will I do? Any impediments? Not a status meeting for management.

Ceremony

Sprint Review

Team demonstrates the increment to stakeholders. Gather feedback. Adapt the backlog. Not a formal sign-off.

Ceremony

Sprint Retrospective

Team reflects on how they worked (not what they built). Identify improvements. "What went well? What to improve?"

Role

Product Owner

Owns the Product Backlog. Maximizes value. Represents stakeholders. One person, not a committee. Decides priority.

Role

Scrum Master

A Servant Leader. Removes impediments. Coaches the team on Scrum practices. Protects the team from outside interference.

Artifact

Product Backlog

An ordered list of everything that is known to be needed in the product. Single source of requirements. Continuously refined.

Concept

Velocity

The amount of work (story points) a team completes in a sprint. Used for forecasting future sprint capacity. Measured over time.

📝 EVM Formulas Quick Reference
Cost Variance
CV = EV − AC
Positive = Under budget. Negative = Over budget.
Schedule Variance
SV = EV − PV
Positive = Ahead of schedule. Negative = Behind schedule.
Cost Performance Index
CPI = EV / AC
>1 = Under budget. <1 = Over budget. Most critical EVM forecasting metric.
Schedule Performance Index
SPI = EV / PV
>1 = Ahead. <1 = Behind. Shows schedule efficiency.
Estimate at Completion
EAC = BAC / CPI
Forecasted total cost. Assumes current CPI trend continues.
Estimate to Complete
ETC = EAC − AC
How much more money is needed to finish.
Variance at Completion
VAC = BAC − EAC
Positive = Under budget at end. Negative = Over budget at end.
To-Complete Perf. Index
TCPI = (BAC−EV) / (BAC−AC)
>1 = Must perform better. <1 = Can afford to slow down. =1 = On track.
